What are industrial lubricants & speciality chemicals?

Industrial lubricants are formulated fluids and greases used to reduce friction, wear and heat in machines and systems. They include hydraulic fluids, compressor oils, turbine oils, gear oils, heat-transfer oils and greases. Specialty chemicals are performance additives and treatment agents used alongside or within lubrication systems — corrosion and scale inhibitors, biocides, defoamers, metalworking additives and more.

Suppliers must provide technical data (TDS), safety data (SDS), recommended maintenance intervals, and evidence of conformity where required (NSF H1 for food contact, OEM approvals, etc.).

Types — what they are and how they're used

Hydraulic oils

Purpose: transmit power and lubricate hydraulic components (pumps, valves, actuators). Typical base oils: mineral (paraffinic), PAO (synthetic) and biodegradable esters for sensitive applications. Key properties: ISO VG viscosity (matching shear and temperature), anti-wear additives (ZDDP or ashless AW), oxidation stability, water separation (demulsibility), and anti-foam.

Selection notes: choose viscosity to maintain film thickness at operating temperature and to limit viscous losses at cold starts. For systems with long life requirements, prioritize oxidation-stable fluids and condition monitoring (particle counts, moisture, viscosity, TAN).

Gear oils

Purpose: protect gear teeth under sliding/rolling contact and heavy loads. Formulations include EP (extreme pressure) additives (sulphur-phosphorus), anti-wear, rust inhibitors and friction modifiers. Gear oils are specified by viscosity classes (ISO VG or SAE) and service categories such as API GL-4 / GL-5 for automotive/industrial gears.

Use-case differences: open gearing or enclosed gearboxes may favor different additive balances—high EP for heavily loaded enclosed gears, lower friction grades for synchronized transmissions.

Compressor & turbine oils

Compressor oils (rotary screw, reciprocating) must resist foaming, varnish, and deposit formation while providing bearing and gear protection. Turbine oils require excellent oxidation stability, low volatility, anti-foam and reliable water separation. Typical tests: RPVOT (rotary pressure vessel oxidation test), FZG or gear-scuffing for gear drives, and centrifugal separation for contaminants.

Heat transfer & thermal oils

Used in closed-loop heating systems (thermal oil heaters), these oils must retain fluidity at low temperature, resist thermal cracking at high temperatures, and have low vapour pressure. Selection focuses on maximum service temperature and expected lifetime (calc via thermal-oxidative stability tests).

Metalworking fluids (cutting fluids & coolants)

Covers straight oils, soluble oils (emulsions), and synthetic or semi-synthetic coolants. Functions: cooling, lubrication, corrosion protection, wash-off of chips, and chip evacuation. Formulation includes extreme-pressure additives for severe cutting, biocides for microbial control in soluble oils, and corrosion inhibitors for post-process protection. Emulsion stability, tramp oil control and bacterial management are essential operations concerns.

Greases (NLGI grades)

Grease is a thickened lubricating oil for bearings and slow-moving interfaces. The NLGI grade (000 to 6) describes consistency — NLGI 2 is the common general-purpose grade for roller and ball bearings. Grease selection considers base oil viscosity, thickener type (lithium complex, calcium sulfonate, polyurea), and additives (EP, anti-corrosion).

Food-grade lubricants

NSF H1 and ISO 21469 certified products are required where incidental food contact is possible. These use approved base oils and additive packages and typically have different odor and taste screening to avoid contamination.

Specialty chemicals — categories and applications

Specialty chemicals complement lubricants or treat systems directly. Below are common classes and practical notes.

Corrosion & scale inhibitors

Used in cooling and process waters; they form protective films, chelate hardness ions, or adjust pH to reduce scaling. Selection depends on feedwater chemistry and permitted treatment chemistries.

Biocides & microbial control

Control bacteria and fungi in water-based systems (coolants and waste streams). Use responsibly with safety and environmental controls; consider non-oxidizing biocides for long-lived systems.

Anti-foam / defoamers

Silicone or non-silicone defoamers reduce foam in emulsions and process tanks—important where air entrainment reduces heat transfer or causes overflow.

Metalworking additives

Additives for cutting oils and coolants—extreme-pressure compounds, lubricity improvers, antioxidants, and anti-wear agents—to increase tool life and surface finish.

Cleaning & degreasing agents

Solvent or aqueous degreasers for pre-treatment. Choose based on substrate compatibility and environmental/safety regulations.

Product-specific blends

Custom formulations for specific OEM needs: anti-wear hydraulic blends, bio-based lubricants, or low-VOC cleaners. We can co-develop where volume or regulatory constraints justify it.

Viscosity grade (ISO VG) reference

ISO VG class describes nominal kinematic viscosity at 40 °C (centistokes). Use the table below as a guideline — final selection must be verified against OEM instructions and temperature-dependent viscosity requirements.

Selection & specification checklist (practical)

When you request a quote, provide the following to get an accurate recommendation and quotation:

  • Operating temperatures (min / max) and ambient temperature range.
  • Duty cycle and continuous vs intermittent loads.
  • Required viscosity at operating temperature (or ISO VG target).
  • Contaminant expectations (water ingress, dust) and filtration arrangements.
  • Compatibility with seals and materials (elastomers, paint, etc.).
  • Regulatory constraints (NSF H1, food contact, biodegradability, REACH).
  • Preferred base oil type (mineral / synthetic / PAO / ester) and desired service life.

We can perform a heat balance / viscosity calculation and propose a lubricant or custom blend when you supply process data and nominal operating points.

Quality assurance & routine testing

Our supply includes a Technical Data Sheet (TDS) and Safety Data Sheet (SDS). For in-service monitoring we recommend periodic testing to track oil condition and wear:

  • Viscosity @40°C and @100°C (confirm no thermal breakdown).
  • TAN (total acid number) and TBN (for alkaline reserve in engine oils).
  • Water content (Karl Fischer) and particle count (ISO 4406/AS4059).
  • Elemental analysis (ICP) to track wear metals and contamination.
  • FTIR or RPVOT for oxidation and varnish precursor assessment.

Packaging, storage & safety

Standard pack sizes: 200 L drums, 20/25 L pails, 1000 L IBCs. Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight, with seals on drums to avoid moisture ingress. Follow SDS for handling, eye/skin protection and spill response. For food-grade products ensure dedicated storage and clean transfer lines to avoid cross-contamination.

Regulatory & sustainability considerations

We supply biodegradable and low-VOC formulations where required and can advise on REACH-related supply constraints. For sensitive applications we provide documented evidence of approvals (NSF, OEM) and end-of-life disposal guidance.
