Heat exchangers — why they matter for emissions

Heat exchangers reduce fuel and electricity consumption by recovering waste heat, improving heat transfer efficiency, and enabling lower-temperature operation. For process industries, modest improvements in heat-transfer performance often translate to significant fuel savings and CO₂ reductions.

Applications & benefits

  • Pre-heating feeds using recovered heat (reduces burner load)
  • Improved boiler/economiser performance through effective recirculation
  • Condensing and latent heat recovery for steam systems
  • Reduced cooling duty via optimized exchanger design

We provide audit → specification → supply services for shell-and-tube, plate, and finned-tube exchangers and optimise for low-pressure drop and high effectiveness to minimise energy penalties.

Carbon emission calculation — methodology

We follow common protocols (GHG Protocol Scope 1 & 2, and applicable national inventory methods) and combine site metering, fuel purchase records, and operational logs to calculate baseline emissions. The workflow below is practical for plant-level accounting.

Step 1 — Data collection

Collect fuel usage (L or m³), electricity meter readings (kWh), process steam consumption, and production throughput for the baseline period. Also capture operating hours and equipment efficiencies.

Step 2 — Convert to activity data

Convert fuel volumes to energy (MJ) using standard calorific values (or supplier-provided CV). Convert electricity kWh to MJ (kWh × 3.6).

Step 3 — Apply emission factors

Multiply activity data by appropriate emission factors (tCO₂e per unit energy) — national factors, IPCC defaults, or supplier certificates for biogenic fuels.

CO₂e = Activity × Emission factor

Quick calculation aid

A simplified approach for a process furnace: measure fuel consumption before (baseline) and after efficiency interventions (e.g., improved heat exchanger). Savings in fuel energy × emission factor = CO₂ saved.

Carbon credits & consultancy

We help clients navigate voluntary and compliance carbon markets, structure emission reduction projects, liaise with certifying bodies, and connect projects to offtake or finance. Typical service path:

Project development

Feasibility assessment, baseline and monitoring plan, identification of additionality and permanence risks. We draft the project design document for validation.

Validation & verification

Coordinate third-party validation against approved methodologies (VCS, Gold Standard, or national schemes). Support verification and issuance workflows.

Market access & finance

Assist with market placement, carbon credit sales, or bundling with energy-efficiency performance contracts. Advice on bank crediting for green loans and sustainability-linked financing.

Sectors we focus on

Food & beverage

Opportunities in waste heat recovery, refrigeration efficiency and fuel switching for boilers.

Pharmaceuticals

High hygiene standards + energy-intensive drying & HVAC — heat recovery yields reliable credits.

Chemicals

Process integration and pinch analysis reduce steam consumption and fugitive emissions.

Plywood & panels

Kiln energy efficiency and biomass co-firing can lower net CO₂ and provide crediting pathways.

Paper & pulp

Industrial-scale steam systems and condensate recovery are major opportunities for emissions reduction.

Standards & methodologies

We work with commonly used protocols and methodologies (GHG Protocol, IPCC guidance, and voluntary standards such as VCS / Gold Standard). For each project we recommend the appropriate methodology and document assumptions transparently for validation.
