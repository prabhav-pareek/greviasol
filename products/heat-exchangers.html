What is a heat exchanger?

A heat exchanger is a device that transfers heat between two or more fluids without mixing them. Typical forms include shell-and-tube, plate and air‑cooled units used across power, oil & gas, HVAC, food, and chemical sectors.

How does it work?

Hot and cold streams flow through separate passages. Heat is transferred across a solid surface by conduction and convection. Counter‑flow arrangements give the highest temperature driving force. Thermal rating commonly uses LMTD or NTU‑effectiveness methods; selection also considers fouling, pressure drop and cleanability.

Products

Box Type Heat Exchangers

Compact, rectangular units where fluids exchange heat through partitioned plates or tubes housed inside a box-shaped casing.

Shell & Tube Heat Exchangers

Shell and Tube Heat Exchangers are engineered for robust performance and high efficiency. These versatile exchangers are ideal for handling fluids at different temperatures and pressures across a wide range of industries.

Plate Heat Exchangers

Consist of multiple thin, corrugated metal plates stacked together, forming channels for two fluids to flow in alternate layers.

Pillow Plate Chiller

Made from two thin stainless-steel sheets laser-welded together and inflated to form a "pillow-like" surface. The design creates internal channels for fluid circulation, offering high heat transfer efficiency, durability, and ease of cleaning

Shell & Plate Heat Exchanger

Compact unit that combines the pressure strength of a shell with the efficiency of welded plate packs inside. The plates create multiple flow channels for effective heat transfer, while the shell provides high mechanical strength, allowing it to handle higher pressures and temperatures compared to conventional plate exchangers.

Finned Tube Heat Exchanger

Tubes are fitted with external metal fins to increase the surface area for heat transfer. The fins improve efficiency by allowing better heat exchange between the fluid inside the tubes and the surrounding medium, making it ideal for compact, high-performance designs.

Common types

Shell & tube

Bundle of tubes in a shell. Handles high pressure/temperature; broad material choices; TEMA styles.

Plate (gasketed / brazed)

Corrugated plates create high turbulence → high efficiency in compact footprint; easy to expand (gasketed).

Air‑cooled (ACHE)

Rejects heat directly to ambient air; no cooling water required; fans and finned tubes.
