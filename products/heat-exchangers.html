Heat Exchangers

Efficient, serviceable and specification-driven exchangers for industrial duty

What is a Heat Exchanger?

A heat exchanger is a device that transfers heat between two or more fluids without mixing them. Typical forms include shell & tube, plate heat exchangers, spiral, box type heat exchangers, plate coils, pillow plate heat exchangers and air-cooled units. These products are used across food, chemical, pharma, power, oil & gas, HVAC, & various other industrial sectors.

Box Type Heat Exchangers

A box type heat exchanger is a customized heat transfer unit where tubes, plates, or coils are enclosed within a rectangular or square box-shaped casing. The design provides flexibility to handle multiple fluid streams, large flow volumes, and non-standard layouts, making it suitable for specialized process requirements.

Key Benefits Include:

  • Design flexibility to accommodate unique process conditions
  • Compact and robust construction for efficient use of space
  • High versatility with options for air, liquid, or gas cooling/heating
  • Ease of integration with existing process systems and ducts

Applications

Box type heat exchangers are used in HVAC systems, industrial ventilation, power plants, food & beverage processing, and other industries where tailored solutions are required for air or process fluid heat recovery.

Shell & Tube Heat Exchangers

The shell & tube heat exchanger is the most widely used and time-tested heat exchanger design. It consists of a bundle of tubes enclosed within a cylindrical shell, with one fluid flowing inside the tubes and another fluid around them. The robust construction makes it suitable for a wide range of pressures, temperatures, and applications.

Key Benefits Include:

  • High durability and reliability for continuous, heavy-duty operations
  • Versatile design adaptable to condensation, evaporation, and heat recovery duties
  • Handles extreme conditions with high pressure and temperature capabilities
  • Ease of cleaning and maintenance through removable tube bundles

Applications

Shell & tube heat exchangers are extensively used in oil & gas, power generation, chemical, petrochemical, marine, and process industries, making them a standard choice where performance and longevity are essential.

Plate Heat Exchangers (PHE)

A plate heat exchanger consists of multiple thin, corrugated plates stacked together to create separate flow channels for hot and cold fluids. The large surface area and turbulent flow patterns deliver highly efficient heat transfer in a compact unit.

Key Benefits Include:

  • Excellent thermal efficiency due to large surface area and counter-current flow
  • Compact and space-saving compared to shell-and-tube exchangers
  • Flexible capacity -- easy to add or remove plates as process demands change
  • Cost-effective with lower material usage for the same duty

Applications

Plate heat exchangers are widely used in HVAC, refrigeration, dairy, food & beverage, pharmaceuticals, chemical, and power industries where high efficiency, hygiene, and easy maintenance are critical.

Pillow Plate Heat Exchanger

A pillow plate heat exchanger is built using two metal sheets laser-welded together in a custom pattern and then inflated to form "pillow-like" channels. This unique design provides efficient heat transfer with high mechanical strength while keeping the unit lightweight and compact.

Key Benefits Include:

  • High thermal efficiency with large surface contact area
  • Flexible design suitable for flat, cylindrical, or custom-shaped surfaces
  • Durability with strong resistance to high pressures and thermal stresses
  • Low fouling and easy cleaning due to smooth surfaces

Applications

Pillow plate heat exchangers are widely used in food & beverage, dairy, brewery, chemical, and energy sectors, especially for applications like cooling tanks, heating vessels, ice banks, and evaporation/condensation duties.

Shell & Plate Heat Exchanger

A shell & plate heat exchanger combines the robustness of a shell-and-tube design with the efficiency of plate technology. It consists of a fully welded plate pack inserted into a cylindrical shell, creating a compact, leak-tight unit capable of handling high pressures and temperatures.

Key Benefits Include:

  • High thermal efficiency with true counter-current flow
  • Compact design with a smaller footprint than traditional shell-and-tube exchangers
  • High pressure and temperature resistance due to fully welded construction
  • Low maintenance with no gaskets, ensuring reliability in demanding conditions

Applications

Shell & plate heat exchangers are commonly used in oil & gas, power generation, chemical processing, and refrigeration industries, particularly where efficiency, durability, and space savings are critical.

Finned Tube Heat Exchanger (FTHE)

A finned tube heat exchanger is designed to enhance heat transfer efficiency between fluids, typically between a liquid and air or gas. It consists of tubes with external fins that increase surface area, allowing more effective heat exchange while keeping the unit compact.

Key Benefits Include:

  • High heat transfer efficiency due to extended finned surface
  • Compact and lightweight design compared to bare tube exchangers
  • Versatile operation for heating, cooling, or condensing applications
  • Cost-effective with reduced material requirement for the same duty

Applications

Finned tube heat exchangers are widely used in HVAC, refrigeration, power plants, chemical processing, and industrial dryers, especially where air-side heat transfer is critical.

Spiral Heat Exchanger (SHE)

A spiral heat exchanger is a highly efficient, compact device designed for handling challenging heat transfer applications involving liquids, gases, vapors, or slurries. Constructed from two metal plates wound into a spiral coil, the design creates single, continuous flow paths for each fluid, ensuring high turbulence and minimal fouling.

Key Benefits Include:

  • High thermal efficiency due to true counter-current flow
  • Compact design with a small footprint compared to shell-and-tube exchangers
  • Excellent fouling resistance as the spiral channels promote self-cleaning
  • Versatility in handling dirty, viscous, or particle-laden fluids

Applications

Spiral heat exchangers are widely used in industries such as chemical, petrochemical, food processing, wastewater treatment, and pulp & paper, especially where reliability and ease of maintenance are critical.
